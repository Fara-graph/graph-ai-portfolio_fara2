About Me

I am Dr. Fara Wagbo Temessadouno, a public health doctor and epidemiologist with extensive experience in disease surveillance, operational research, and humanitarian project coordination. Over the past decade, I have worked with Médecins Sans Frontières (MSF) and Epicentre as an epidemiology advisor, project manager, and researcher across Africa.

I hold a Doctor of Medicine from Gamal Abdel Nasser University in Conakry, and master’s degrees in Public Health and Epidemiology from the Universities of Aix-Marseille, Toulouse III, and Bordeaux. My research includes studies on infectious diseases, HIV/TB co-infection, and COVID‑19 seroprevalence.

My current professional interests include the integration of AI tools into research, data analysis, and global health workflows to improve decision-making and efficiency in humanitarian contexts.

About the Course

Generative AI for Work & Research is an 8-week live cohort course developed by the GRAPH Network at the University of Geneva. It focuses on practical, no-code AI tools for automating work, research, and data analysis.

  • Module 1: Websites, Documents & Presentations — Learn LLM fundamentals and generate research outputs.
  • Module 2: AI‑Automated Data Analysis — Use ChatGPT Code Interpreter, Julius, and TheBricks for analysis and reporting.
  • Module 3: GenAI for Research & Text Analysis — Apply AI to literature review, sentiment analysis, and synthesis.
  • Module 4: Custom GPTs and Chatbots — Build and integrate intelligent assistants for research tasks.
  • Module 5: AI for Task Automation — Develop autonomous agents for workflow automation.
  • Module 6: GenAI for Communication & Outreach — Design visual and written communication materials with AI.
  • Module 7: Capstone Project & Showcase — Apply all acquired skills to build an AI‑powered research solution.
